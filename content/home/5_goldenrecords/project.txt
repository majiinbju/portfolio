Title: Fool's Gold

----

Category: Interactive Design

----

Year: 2020

----

Link: https://goldenrecord.glitch.me/

----

What: Fool's Gold is an interactive experience detailing the relationship between minorities and the United States government. During the Space Race, a lot of economic resources were allocated toward the betterment of space-science in an effort to combat the USSR's rising power as a giant in science. As a result, the government ended up ignoring the plight of many of its minorities. This website aims to critique these poor decisions by the United States by repropriating one of the greatest marvels in space science: The Golden Records.

----

Why: This project was inspired by the song Whitey on the Moon by Gil Scott-Heron. The lyrics speak about a poor man lamenting about his socio-economic struggles due to negligence by the government. As the user scrolls through the website, the Golden Records spin in response. Each section of the website adopts the role of a "track" on an album. These tracks detail different aspects of the political climate that contributed towards this.

----

How: This project calls to the genesis of rapid development in science. As such, it is designed to allude to the Streamline era of design. The typeface employed is OCR-A, the first typeface that was legible to both humans and computers. There is little colour save for the goldenrod employed on the lyrics, buttons and the records.
