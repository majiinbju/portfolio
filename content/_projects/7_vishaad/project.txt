Title: Vishaad

----

Category: Book Design + Illustration

----

Year: 2020

----

Link: 

----

What: Vishaad is the second installment in a series of experiments on understanding Nostalgia. Vishaad is a Hindi word that means vivid. As the book poses questions about Nostalgia, it navigates through various aspects of media. It begs the question as to why so many design trends and creative endeavours have been harkening back to the past. Shows like Stranger Things, games like Katana Zero and the countless remakes in Hollywood signal a clear affection to the past. Moreover, it has become increasingly popular for one to own items such as polaroid cameras and record players even though the modern phone is a much more convenient object.

----

Why: Vishaad is set in <a href="https://velvetyne.fr/fonts/bluu/" class="link">Bluu Next</a> and supplemented by Arial. The two fonts clash to create a simple, futuristic aesthetic. Heavily inspired by art of the post-war Japanese period by artists such as Koichi Sato. The book itself tries to blend the metaphysical nature of nostalgia with questions of its own. The book is accompanied by a set of such posters which are very reminiscent of the aforementioned art.

----

How: Unlike its predecessor, it doesn't adopt a modular and technological approach, rather it tries to mimic how creatives then envisioned the world. The book aims to elicit the feelings of one being in an outer-space environment. Organised and rigid. Vishaad also analyses the contemporary trend of Vaporwave, which was a huge source of inspiration for this project. It signals an unequivocal affinity toward Nostalgia that the current generation has adopted.
